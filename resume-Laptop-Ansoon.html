Personal Projects

Robot Card Dealer Machine

A product created during the 2024 annual hackathon, Build18, hosted by Carnegie Mellon University's Electrical and Computer Engineering Department. Me, along with a team of first-year students tackled on a goal for this year's hackathon: designing a robotic card dealer equipped with a shuffling algorithm. Assigned roles and scheduled meets were coordinated which involved tackling bugs in code while assembling the machine within a week's time.

BlotShooter Game

Inspired by my coursework at Carnegie Mellon University's Fundamentals of Programming and Computer Science course, I've crafted a captivating mini-game. In this dynamic experience, players engage in a thrilling shootout, aiming to land blots on their opponent. The game boasts randomly generated wall obstacles, injecting unpredictability and excitement into each round. A special feature of the game is the introduction of a powerball, strategically placed to grant its discoverer an edge in the duel.
